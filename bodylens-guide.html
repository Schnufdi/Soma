The floating coach knows where you are
The chat button is visible on every page. It reads the page you're on and your full profile before answering.
What to ask
On the training page: "Should I add a set to bench this week?" — it knows your week number and phase.
On the food page: "I'm craving bread at 4pm — what do I do?" — it knows your trigger foods.
On the science page: "How does this apply to me specifically?" — it connects the science to your profile.
Anywhere: "I haven't been sleeping well this week" — it will offer to update your profile and adjust recommendations.
When committed: "What would a 24-hour fast do for me right now?" — it will explain the mechanism and whether the timing is right.
The proactive feature
If you mention something new — a new injury, changed sleep, a supplement you've started — the coach will ask if you want to save it to your profile. Your profile updates in real time. The next programme block reflects everything you've told it.
Tap the expand icon (⤢) in the coach panel for the full question library — 7 categories, 5 questions each, all pre-loaded with your context. Browse what's available when you're not sure what to ask.
The 4-week rhythm — how the programme progresses
Each 4-week block is a progression arc. The exercises stay the same — what changes is the intensity and volume. Do the check-in at the end of week 4 and the next block rebuilds from where you actually are.
Week 1
Foundation
Learn the movement patterns. Establish baseline loads. RPE 6-7. Focus on technique, not performance.
Week 2
Build
Add one working set per primary lift. Loads increase where form was solid. Volume up 10-15%.
Week 3
Peak
Highest intensity of the block. RPE 8-9 on main lifts. This is the week that drives adaptation.
Week 4
Deload
50% volume reduction. Same or slightly reduced loads. Let the adaptation consolidate. Then check in.
Things that make the biggest difference

Track what you actually ate
The macro cup only works if you log. One tap = "ate this." Two words = "had a wrap instead." Claude estimates the macros. Takes 3 seconds per meal.
📝
Log your training weights
After each exercise, note what you lifted. The benchmark marker updates. Next week you know exactly what to beat. Progressive overload only works if you track it.
🎯
Do the level quiz
Tap the training block on your first training day. The 5-question quiz sets your level accurately across all exercises. Your benchmark markers actually mean something after this.
⏰
Open Today first, every day
The daily plan is built fresh each morning based on your programme. It tells you everything you need for the day. Make it the first thing you check, not the weather.
🚀
Add one accelerator at a time
The accelerators page has 24 options. The daily plan surfaces one each day. Start with the easy wins — post-meal walks, morning light, NSDR. Stack them one per week.
💬
Tell the coach things you notice
If something changes — sleep, stress, a new ache — tell the coach. It will offer to update your profile. Your programme adapts to your real life, not a static snapshot from day one.
