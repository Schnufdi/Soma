Auth gate — lock the product
Every page is accessible without login. The data layer is live. The programme generation is real. A paying product cannot be fully open. One redirect in nav.js is all it takes.

🧠 Intelligence upgrades — the feedback loop 5 ideas
The infrastructure is complete. These ideas convert stored data into adaptive coaching — the architectural difference between a premium tool and a genuinely intelligent system.
Intelligence Moat
Weekly reconciliation pass — the feedback loop
Every Monday morning, a Claude call reads the last 7 days of day_logs and week_ledger. It compares planned vs actual training, energy trends, and RPE signals. It outputs programme adjustments and a coaching narrative. The data is there. This call isn't.

This is the single most important intelligence upgrade. Right now BodyLens stores everything and adapts nothing. A user who logs energy 2/5 for five days running gets the same plan next week. A user who skips every Pull session gets the same plan. The weekly reconciliation pass is what converts a logging app into a coaching system. Once it exists, BodyLens becomes something no competitor can replicate quickly — because the quality of the adaptation is a function of the data, and the data is accumulating every day.

Intelligence
Contradiction detection pre-pass
Before generating the daily plan, a short Claude call scans the profile for tension: caloric deficit + high training volume + poor sleep + high stress. When tensions exist, the day's coaching note surfaces the conflict directly instead of ignoring it.

The most common coaching failure is advice that ignores context. A 500kcal deficit, 4 heavy training sessions, 5.5h sleep average, and high stress should produce a different plan than the same profile with 8h sleep. Right now BodyLens treats these identically. The contradiction detection pass costs ~$0.008 per day and changes the quality of every coaching interaction for users in suboptimal states — which is most users, most of the time.

Critique 7/10
Does the daily plan actually adapt? Not yet.
The daily plan is contextually generated but not adaptive. It generates a great day based on your profile — but the same profile generates the same day regardless of last week's training. User who logged energy 2/5 for five days gets the same plan. This is the defining gap.

The fix is the weekly reconciliation pass (see Intelligence section). Until it exists, BodyLens is a sophisticated daily plan generator, not an adaptive coaching system. The distinction matters to serious athletes — the audience most willing to pay £39.99/month. The data infrastructure now supports it. The reasoning layer doesn't exist yet. This is a 4-hour build that changes the product category.

Most important fix 4 hours to build
The gap: Plan generation reads profile but not logs. Same profile = same plan.
Build the weekly reconciliation pass. In daily plan generation, pass last 7 days of day_logs as additional context. Add to the system prompt: "Recent training history: [last 7 days]. If you detect fatigue, low energy trend, or missed sessions — adjust today's intensity accordingly and explain why in the coach note." This is a context addition, not a new feature. It can be done in 2 hours.
Critique 5/10
Scientific authority without scientific humility
The supplement recommendations, sleep science, and some HRV guidance are presented with more certainty than the research supports. The product needs a tiered evidence framework. Every claim should carry a label: established, emerging, or anecdotal. Right now everything sounds equally certain.

Counterintuitively, showing uncertainty increases trust. A product that says "this is established science (7 RCTs)" and "this is emerging (2 preliminary studies)" is more credible than one that presents everything with equal confidence. Sophisticated users — doctors, scientists, serious athletes — are immediately sceptical of uniform certainty. An evidence tier system is a 2-day build across the science pages. It's also genuinely honest, which is rare in health products and immediately differentiating.

Trust-building 2 days across science pages
The gap: All claims presented with equivalent authority. No source tier labelling.
Add evidence tier badges to every factual claim in science pages and supplement recommendations: 🟢 Established (meta-analyses, multiple RCTs) / 🟡 Emerging (preliminary RCTs, observational) / ⚪ Anecdotal (community practice, case studies). Audit the most confident claims first — sauna, creatine timing, HRV guidance, body fat estimation. Remove false precision on body fat assessments. Add "Evidence note" expandable sections on supplement cards. 2 days of systematic audit across 15 science pages.
Critique 4/10
Edge-case handling is dangerously absent
A user with hypertension. A user with disordered eating history requesting restriction. Someone with chronic lower back pain asking about deadlift load. None handled with appropriate caution. The system routes to authority when it should route to caution. In a health product, this is a liability.

Every health product that has scaled has been burned by edge-case failures. A user who receives overconfident caloric restriction advice while in a disordered eating episode, or a training load recommendation that aggravates an undiagnosed cardiac condition — these are the failures that generate regulatory scrutiny and press coverage. The fix is a pre-pass classifier on the user's health conditions field, routing to appropriate caution language when red flags are detected. Not a full medical screening — just pattern matching on known high-risk terms.

Risk reduction 1 day to build
The gap: No risk detection. System generates authoritative advice regardless of health context.

Suggest they discuss training adjustments with their physician." Add medical disclaimer to bottom of every coaching output. These are legal requirements in health products, not optional.
